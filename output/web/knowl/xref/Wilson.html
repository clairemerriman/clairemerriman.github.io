Theorem 72. Wilsonâ€™s Theorem.

Let \(p\) be a prime number. Then
\begin{equation*} (p-1)!\equiv -1\pmod{p}. \end{equation*}

Proof.

When \(p=2,\) \((2-1)!=1\equiv -1 \pmod{2}.\) Now consider \(p\) an odd prime. By Corollary 2.8, each \(a=1,2,\dots,p-1\) has a unique multiplicative inverse modulo \(p.\) Lemma 2.10 says the only elements that are their own multiplicative inverse are \(1\) and \(p-1\text{.}\) Thus \((p-2)!\) is the product of \(1\) and \(\frac{p-3}{2}\) pairs of \(a,a^\prime\) where \(a a^\prime\equiv 1\pmod{p}.\) Therefore,
\begin{align*} (p-2)! \amp \equiv 1\pmod{p}\\ (p-1)! \amp \equiv p-1\equiv -1 \pmod{p}. \end{align*}
