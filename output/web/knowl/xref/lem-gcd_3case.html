Lemma 39.

Let \(a,b,c\in\Z,\) with \(a\neq 0\text{.}\) Then \((a,b,c)=((a,b),c).\)

Proof.

Let \(a,b,c\in\Z,\) with \(a\neq 0\text{.}\) Define \(d=(a,b,c)\) and \(e=((a,b),c).\) We will show that \(d\mid e\) and \(e\mid d\text{.}\) Since the greatest common divisor is positive, we can conclude that \(d=e\)
.
Since \(d=(a,b,c),\) we know \(d\mid a, d\mid b,\) and \(d\mid c\text{.}\) By the lemma we are about to prove, \(d\mid (a,b)\text{.}\) Thus, \(d\) is a common divisor of \((a,b)\) and \(c\text{,}\) so \(d\mid e\text{.}\)
Since \(e=((a,b),c)\text{,}\) \(e\mid (a,b)\) and \(e\mid c\text{.}\) Since \(e\mid (a,b),\) we know \(e\mid a\) and \(e\mid b\) by the same lemma. Thus, \(e\) is a common divides of \(a,b\) and \(c\)
