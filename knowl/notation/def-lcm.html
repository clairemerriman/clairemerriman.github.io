Definition 179. Least common multiple.

If \(a\mid c\) and \(b\mid c\) then \(c\) is a common multiple of \(a\) and \(b\text{.}\)
The least positive common multiple of \(a\) and \(b\) is called the least common multiple of \(a\) and \(b\) and is denoted \(\lcm[a,b]\) or just \([a,b]\text{.}\)
If we want the least common multiple of several integers at once we denote that by \(\lcm[b_1,b_2,b_3,\dots,b_n]\) or \([b_1,b_2,b_3,\dots,b_n]\text{.}\)
