Definition 50. Congruence modulo \(m\).

Let \(a,b,m\in\Z\) with \(m\gt 0.\) We say that \(a\) is congruent to \(b\) modulo \(m\) and write \(a \equiv b \pmod{m}\) if \(m\mid b-a\text{,}\) and \(m\) is said to be the modulus of the congruence. The notation \(a\not\equiv b\pmod m\) means \(a\) is not congruent to \(b\) modulo \(m,\) or \(a\) is incongruent to \(b\) modulo \(m\text{.}\)
