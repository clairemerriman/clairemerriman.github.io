Definition 23. Greatest common divisor, relatively prime.

If \(a\mid b\) and \(a\mid c\) then \(a\) is a common divisor of \(b\) and \(c\text{.}\)
If at least one of \(b\) and \(c\) is not \(0\text{,}\) the greatest (positive) number among their common divisors is called the greatest common divisor of \(a\) and \(b\) and is denoted \(gcd(a,b)\) or just \((a,b)\text{.}\)
If \(gcd(a,b)=1\text{,}\) we say that \(a\) and \(b\) are relatively prime.
If we want the greatest common divisor of several integers at once we denote that by \(\gcd(b_1,b_2,b_3,\dots,b_n)\) or \((b_1,b_2,b_3,\dots,b_n)\text{.}\)
