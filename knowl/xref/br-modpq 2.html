Let \(p,q\) be distinct primes. Prove that \(p^{q-1}+q^{p-1}\equiv 1 \pmod{pq}.\)
Proof.
Let \(p,q\) be distinct primes. Then \(q^{p-1}\equiv 1\pmod{p}\) and \(p^{q-1}\equiv 1\pmod{q}\) by Fermatâ€™s Little Theorem, and \(p^{q-1}\equiv 0\pmod{p}\) and \(q^{p-1}\equiv 0\pmod{q}\) by definition.
Thus, \(p^{q-1}+q^{p-1}\equiv {1} \pmod{p}\) and \(p^{q-1}+q^{p-1}\equiv {1} \pmod{q}\) by addition.
Thus, \(p\mid p^{q-1}+q^{p-1}-1\) and \(q\mid p^{q-1}+q^{p-1}-1\) by definition. Since \((p,q)=1,\) \(pq\mid p^{q-1}+q^{p-1}-1.\) Thus, \(p^{q-1}+q^{p-1}\equiv {1} \pmod{pq}\)
