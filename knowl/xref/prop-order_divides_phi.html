Proposition 101. Proposition 5.1.

Let \(a,m\in\Z\) with \(m\gt 0\) and \((a,m)=1.\) Then \(a^n\equiv 1\pmod{m}\) for some positive integer \(n\) if and only if \(\ord_m a\mid n.\) In particular, \(\ord_m a\mid \phi(m).\)

Let \(a,m\in\Z\) with \(m\gt 0\) and \((a,m)=1.\)
(\(\Rightarrow\))
We want to show if \(a^n\equiv 1\pmod{m}\) for some positive integer \(n,\) then \(\ord_m a\mid n.\)
By the Division Algorithm, there exist unique integers \(q, r\) such that \(n=(\ord_m a)q+r\) and \(0\leq r\lt \ord_m a.\) Thus,
\begin{align*} 1 \amp \equiv a^n \equiv a^{(\ord_m a)q+r}\pmod{m}\\ \amp \equiv (a^{(\ord_m a)})^q a^r \equiv a^r\pmod{m} \end{align*}
