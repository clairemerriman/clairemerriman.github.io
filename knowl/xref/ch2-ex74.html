Problem 208. Strayer Chapter 2, Exercise 74.

Let \(p\) be an odd prime number. Prove that
\begin{equation*} \left\{\frac{-(p-1)}{2}, \frac{-(p-3)}{2},\dots, -2,-1,1,2,\dots, \frac{p-2}{2},\frac{p-1}{2}\right\} \end{equation*}
is a reduced residue system modulo \(p\text{.}\)
