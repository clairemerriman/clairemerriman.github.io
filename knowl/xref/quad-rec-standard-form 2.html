Theorem 146. Law of Quadratic Reciprocity.

Let \(p\) and \(q\) be distinct primes. Then
\begin{equation*} \legendre{p}{q}\legendre{q}{p}=(-1)^{(p-1)/2\ (q-1)/2}= \begin{cases} 1, \amp \textnormal{ if } p\equiv 1\pmod{4} \textnormal{ or } q\equiv 1\pmod{4}\\ -1, \amp \textnormal{ if } p\equiv q\equiv 3\pmod{4}. \end{cases} \end{equation*}
