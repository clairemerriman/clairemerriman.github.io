Lemma 69. Lemma 2.10.

Let \(p\) be a prime number and \(a\in\Z.\) Then \(a\) is its own inverse modulo \(m\) if and only if \(a\equiv \pm 1\pmod{p}.\)

Proof.

Let \(p\) be a prime number and \(a\in\Z.\) Then \(a\) is its own inverse modulo \(m\) if and only if \(a^2\equiv 1 \pmod{p}\) if and only if \(p\mid a^2-1=(a-1)(a+1).\) Since \(p\) is prime, \(p\mid a-1\) or \(a+1\) by Lemma 1.14. Thus, \(a\equiv\pm 1\pmod p.\)
