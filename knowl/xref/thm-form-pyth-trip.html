Theorem 165. Theorem 6.3.

There are infinitely many primitive Pythagorean triples \(x,y,z\) with \(y\) even. Furthermore, they are given precisely by the equations
\begin{align*} x\amp =m^2-n^2\\ y\amp =2mn\\ z\amp =m^2+n^2 \end{align*}
where \(m,n\in\mathbb{Z}, m\gt n\gt 0, (m,n)=1\) and exactly one of \(m\) and \(n\) is even.
