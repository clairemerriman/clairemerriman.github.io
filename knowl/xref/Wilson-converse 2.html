Proposition 73. Proposition 2.12.

Let \(n\) be a positive integer. If \((n-1)!\equiv -1\pmod{n},\) then \(n\) is prime.

Proof.

Let \(a\) and \(b\) be positive integers where \(ab=n.\) It suffices to show that if \(1\leq a \lt n,\) then \(a=1.\) If \(a=n,\) then \(b=1.\) If \(1\leq a \lt n,\) then \(a\mid (n-1)!\) by the definition of factorial. Then \((n-1)!\equiv -1\pmod{n}\) implies \(a\mid (n-1)!+1\) by transitivity of division. Thus, \(a\mid (n-1)!+1-(n-1)!=1\) by linear combination and \(a=1.\) Therefore, the only positive factors of \(n\) are \(1\) and \(n,\) so \(n\) is prime.
