Proof of Division Algorithm.

Let \(a,b\in\Z\) with \(b\gt 0\text{.}\) Define \(q=\left\lfloor\frac{a}{b}\right\rfloor\) and \(r=a-b\left\lfloor\frac{a}{b}\right\rfloor\text{.}\) Then \(a=bq+r\) by rearranging the equation. Now we need to show \(0\leq r\lt b\text{.}\)
Since \(x-1\lt\lfloor x\rfloor\leq x\) by Strayer, Lemma 1.3, we have
\begin{equation*} \frac{a}{b}-1\lt\left\lfloor\frac{a}{b}\right\rfloor\leq\frac{a}{b}. \end{equation*}
Multiplying all terms by \(-b\text{,}\) we get
\begin{equation*} -a+b\gt -b\left\lfloor\frac{a}{b}\right\rfloor\geq-a. \end{equation*}
Adding \(a\) to every term gives
\begin{equation*} b\gt a-b\left\lfloor\frac{a}{b}\right\rfloor\geq 0. \end{equation*}
By the definition of \(r\text{,}\) we have shown \(0\leq r \lt b\text{.}\)
Finally, we need to show that \(q\) and \(r\) are unique. Assume there exist \(q_1,q_2,r_1,r_2\in\Z\) with
\begin{equation*} a=bq_1+r_1, \quad 0\leq r_1\lt b \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} a=bq_2+r_2, \quad 0\leq r_2\lt b. \end{equation*}
We need to show \(q_1=q_2\) and \(r_1=r_2\text{.}\) We can subtract the two equations from each other.
\begin{align*} a \amp =bq_1+r_1, \\ \\ 0 \amp =bq_1+r_1-bq_2-r_2=b(q_1-q_2)+(r_1-r_2) . \end{align*}
Rearranging, we get \(b(q_1-q_2)=r_2-r_1\text{.}\) Thus, \(b\mid r_2-r_1\text{.}\) From rearranging the inequalities:
\begin{align*} 0 \amp \leq r_2\lt b\\ \\ -b \amp \lt r_2-r_1\lt b. \end{align*}
Thus, the only way \(b\mid r_2-r_1\) is that \(r_2-r_1=0\) and thus \(r_1=r_2\text{.}\) Now, \(0=b(q_1-q_2)+(r_1-r_2)\) becomes \(0=b(q_1-q_2)\text{.}\) Since we assumed \(b\gt 0\text{,}\) we have that \(q_1-q_2=0\text{.}\)
