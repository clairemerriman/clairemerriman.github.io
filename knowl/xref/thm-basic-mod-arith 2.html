Theorem 55. Restatement of Strayer Propositions 2.1, 2.4, and 2.5.

Let \(a,b,c,d,m\in\Z\) with \(m\gt 0.\) Then:
  1. \(a\equiv b \pmod{m}\) and \(b\equiv c \pmod{m}\) implies \(a\equiv c \pmod{m}\)
  2. \(a\equiv b \pmod{m}\) and \(c\equiv d \pmod{m}\) implies \(a+c \equiv b+d \pmod{m}\)
  3. \(a\equiv b\pmod{m}\) and \(c\equiv d \pmod{m}\) implies \(ac\equiv bd \pmod{m}\text{.}\)
  4. \(a\equiv b \pmod{m}\) and \(d\mid m\text{,}\) \(d\gt 0\) implies \(a\equiv b \pmod{d}\)
  5. \(a\equiv b \pmod{m}\) implies \(ac\equiv bc \pmod{mc}\) for \(c\gt 0\text{.}\)

Proof.

Let \(a,b,c,d,m\in\Z\) with \(m\gt 0.\)
  1. Assume \(a\equiv b \pmod{m}\) and \(b\equiv c \pmod{m}.\) Then using the second definition of equivalence modulo \(m\) 51, there exists \(q_1,q_2,q_3,r\in\Z\) such that
    \begin{align*} a \amp =mq_1+r, \ 0\leq r\lt m,\\ b \amp =mq_2+r, \ 0\leq r\lt m,\\ c \amp =mq_3+r, \ 0\leq r\lt m. \end{align*}
    Thus, \(a\) and \(c\) have the same remainder when divided by \(m,\) so \(a\equiv c\pmod m.\)
  3. Assume \(a\equiv b \pmod{m}\) and \(c\equiv d \pmod{m}.\) Then by the third definition of equivalence modulo \(m\), there exists \(j,k\in\Z\) such that \(b=a+mj\) and \(d=c+mk.\) Thus,
    \begin{align*} b+d \amp =a+c+m(j+k), \qquad\qquad\textnormal{and}\\ bd \amp = ac+m(ak+cj+mjk). \end{align*}
    Thus, \(a+c\equiv b+d\pmod m\) and \(ac=bd\pmod m.\)
  4. Assume \(a\equiv b \pmod{m},\) and \(d\gt 0\) with \(d\mid m.\) From the first definition of equivalence modulo \(m,\) 50 \(m\mid b-a\text{.}\) Since division is transitive, \(d\mid b-a,\) so \(a\equiv b\pmod d.\)
  5. Assume \(a\equiv b \pmod{m},\) and \(c\gt 0.\) From the third definition of equivalence modulo \(m,\) there exists \(k\in\Z\) such that \(b=a+mk.\) Thus, \(bc=ac+mck,\) so \(ac\equiv bc \pmod{mc}.\)
