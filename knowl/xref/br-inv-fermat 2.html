Prove: Let \(p\) be a prime. If \(a,k\in\Z\) with \(p\nmid a\) and \(0\leq k\lt p,\) then \(a^{p-k}\) is the multiplicative inverse of \(a^k\) modulo \(p\text{.}\)
Solution.
Let \(p\) be a prime. If \(a\in\Z\) with \(p\nmid a,\) then by Fermatâ€™s Little Theorem, \(a^{p-1}\equiv 1\pmod{p.}\) If \(k\in\Z\) with \(0\leq k\lt p,\) then \(a^{p-1}=a^{p-k}a^k.\) Thus, \(a^{p-k}a^k\equiv 1\pmod{p}.\)
